About

Jonnie Whittington, born at Six Mile Creek in Tampa, Florida, grew up in five states scattered across America. She sings, plays and teaches piano. The author of ten books, fifty songs, ten dramas, and a number of poems, Jonnie birthed three women’s seminars and with ministering teams took them over the state of Florida. She speaks at women’s conferences, churches, and banquets and is a comedienne/entertainer.


Jonnie is mother to three, grandmother to eight and great grandmother to one. Her husband, father, son and son-in-law are ministers.

She ran to turn on the water while I made a beeline for Daddy. He hobbled out of the church as fast as he could on his short leg, and squirted water on the roof just as Mama came out the parsonage door. “Yates, I smell smoke,” she yelled. “It’s all right, Dollie. Kidd's Daughter Becomes a Woman

He’s nine and I’m eight when he tells his mother that when we grow up he’s going to marry me. Now he’s dead. All my hopes for the future are gone; buried under a tree in the cemetery plot where his young, broken body lies. With my big ears, stringy hair and nose too large for my face, nobody else will want me. Who would? I’ll just be an old maid like the decrepit looking woman on the cards my sister, Bobbie, and I play. At thirteen things turn around; Boys look my way. Cute boys, some of them . . . At fifteen, Mama catches me kissing one . . . uh oh! And then there’s the terrifying day the man pulls a gun on us . . . Will I survive to become a woman?

The Kidd Family Series’ third book takes us back to the 1950’s. We travel miles facing adventure after adventure along the bumpy road of growing up.
